What is it? Far from being an elusive concept comprised of enigmatic technologies and difficult to understand terms, Cloud Computing is at its core the delivery of computing services over the internet or "cloud" ("What Is Cloud Computing?").

Is it the best option for me? Cloud Computing offers many compelling benefits when compared with traditional on-site data centers. In fact, many small companies today are already leveraging the numerous advantages of cloud computing to maintain a competitive edge. Here are 9 reasons cloud computing would be good for your business ("10 advantages of cloud computing"):

  1. Allows for greater flexibility
  2. Access to robust data security & recovery
  3. No need for software updates
  4. You pay for only what you need
  5. Greatly increases mobility of workforce
  6. Facilitates collaboration efforts between employees
  7. Has built-in document version control
  8. Provides a competitive advantage
  9. Good for sustainability

Is It New? Cloud Computing has been around for quite some time now. The first working concept was essentially born out of a DARPA project in 1963 with the goal of creating a technology that enabled simultaneous use of what would be considered today a very rudimentary computer system. Today, Cloud Computing is far, far more advanced (Foote, "A Brief History").
